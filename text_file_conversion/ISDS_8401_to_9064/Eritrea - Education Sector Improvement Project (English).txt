I.A.2. Project Objectives:
The main Project objective is to increase enrolment in basic education, particularly for disadvantaged
children, while improving the quality of basic and secondary education. The Government of Eritrea has
committed to achieving the Millennium Development Goals, including completion of a full course of
primary schooling for all children and gender equality at all levels and the Project will assist in helping
the Government to establish a firm basis for full achievement of the MDGs.
I.A.3. Project Description:
The Project will support increased enrolment in basic education and improved quality in basic and
secondary education through five major components:
(i)    Expanding coverage through construction of classrooms for elementary and middle school and
support for bursaries for girls;
(ii)   Improving quality through construction of secondary schools to reduce overcrowding,
reforming the curriculum to ensure relevance and supporting textbook production and
distribution for the new curriculum;
reforming assessment and examinations to ensure that examinations support normal progression
while national assessments measure quality of education;
reforming teacher development to meet longer term needs at all levels and implementing an
immediate program of support for middle school teachers;
providing opportunities for relevant inservice training to all elementary and middle school
teachers; and
exploring the potential for World Links for development to utilize internet for pedagogical
purposes in selected secondary schools.
(iii)  Enhancing capacity through a series of planning studies and the implementation of the results of
a capacity analysis and plan for MOE headquarters and Zobas;

II.D.la. Describe any safeguard issues and impacts associated with the proposed project. Identify and
describe any potential large scale, significant and/or irreversible impacts.

II.D.3. Describe arrangement for the borrower to address safeguard issues
The borrower will prepare an Environmental and Social Management Framework, including a
