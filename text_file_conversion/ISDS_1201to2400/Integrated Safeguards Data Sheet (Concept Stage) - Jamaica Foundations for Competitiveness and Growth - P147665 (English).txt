                           B. Project Objectives
                           To strengthen the enabling environment for private sector competitiveness to help Jamaica unleash
                           productivity and jobs growth. This will be done by enhancing competition in the business
                           environment, facilitating private investment and supporting training and financing of SMEs.
                           C. Project Description
                           The proposed operation will focus on four complementary areas related to building competition and
                           capabilities essential for unleashing private sector productivity upgrading and growth in Jamaica.
                           This multi-pronged programmatic approach is needed to effectively address the interlinked
Public Disclosure Copy




                           challenges constraining the Jamaican private sector. Local enterprises will be incentivized and
                           enabled to catch up to the global knowledge frontier through:
                           (1) Enhanced competition through improvements in the business environment, leading to more
                           efficient resource allocation across all enterprises and industries over time. This will include support
                           to spur competition through regulatory reform and implementation assistance; customs and other
                           trade facilitation reforms; and support to implement other investment climate regulatory reforms.
                           (2) The attraction of large anchor investors to stimulate competition, directly generate jobs, diffuse
                           global practices and provide greater international market access for local SMEs. This will include a
                           project preparation facility and investment generation support to facilitate strategic investments with
                           private participation such as in logistics infrastructure and divestment of government assets in the
                           tourism industry.
                           (3) Skills and finance support to local SMEs to ensure that they benefit from the improved business
                           environment and the global knowledge and market access provided by the anchor investors. This will
                           include a skills and knowledge transfer business training program and a SME credit line.
                           A final component will facilitate monitoring of the results and impact of these three components,
                           ensuring continuous learning and improvements over time.
                           D. Project location and salient physical characteristics relevant to the safeguard
                           analysis (if known)
                           Project activities may take place anywhere in Jamaica. Specific SMEs to be supported with training
                           and finance, as well as strategic investments facilitated through feasibility and other studies and TA,
                           will be determined during project implementation based on the most viable value chain linkages and
                           projects identified for support.
                           E. Borrowers Institutional Capacity for Safeguard Policies
                           The Government of Jamaica's (GOJ) institutional capacity for safeguard policies is considered high.
Public Disclosure Copy




                           GOJ policies relevant to the Project Preparation Facility (PPF) such as the PPP and Divestment
                           Policies require compliance with environmental and social impact policies as required by the
                           National Environmental and Planning Agency. The Development Bank of Jamaica (DBJ) as the lead
                           technical agency for the PPF requires that all projects supported by DBJ comply fully with these
                           policies. DBJ will also implement the lines of credit to financial institutions for on-lending to SMEs,
                           and has experience requiring that relevant GOJ environmental legislation and regulations are
                           complied with in the lending operations. Further investigation of institutional capacity for safeguard
                           policies will be explored during preparation missions.
