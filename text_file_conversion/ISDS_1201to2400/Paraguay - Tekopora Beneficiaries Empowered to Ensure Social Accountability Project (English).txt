2. Project Objectives:

The Project's objective of this project is to develop a model for civil society-government
partnership for more transparent and accountable implementation of Paraguay's Tekopora
Conditional Cash Transfer (CCT) program.

3. Project Description:

Component 1: Strengthening the beneficiaries' voice, civil society oversight.

The objective of this component is to strengthen the beneficiaries'.voice and the capacity of Civil
Society to implement social accountability mechanisms. Overall, the component will focus in the
collection of information from beneficiaries, developing the institutional arrangements for the
flow of information and building a constituency to support the program.
   (a) Design a sample of beneficiary families to obtain a national representation of families
       involved in Tekoporii.
   (b) Involve I 0 districts Citizen Round Tables (CRT) in those zones where sample
       beneficiaries resides. Sign working agreements with I 0 municipals and four departmental
       authorities in sample territories.
   (c) Sign working agreements with SAS, the Minister of Health and the Ministry of Education
       to engage healthcare and education services providers at the local level.
   (d) Build a coalition with opinion leaders, the press, the private sector and political parties to
       monitor, protect and promote the program.
   (e) Design survey (similar to Citizen Report Cards) and train beneficiaries and 10 Citizen
       Round Tables (CRT) on the use of information.
   (f) Conduct survey (similar to Citizen Report Cards) of beneficiaries.
   (g) Develop a communication strategy to promote Tekoporii, including promoting debates on
       conditional-cash transfer programs and other welfare programs.

Component 2: Improving the quality, effectiveness and efficiency of the cash transfer
program.

The objective of this component is to develop a plan and a monitoring system to gradually
improve the quality, effectiveness, efficiency and targeting of Tekoporii. Overall, the component
will focus on the development of a dashboard and its monitoring.

   (a) Create a technical working group with the Secretaria de Accion Social (SAS), the
       Ministry of Health (MoH) and the Ministry of Education (MoE) and Citizen Round Table
       to agree in actions and their sequencing to improve Tekoporii.
   (b) Sign agreement between the above institutions with specific goals and implementation
       schedule for the improvement of Tekoporii. These agreements will be turn into dashboard
       to be monitored by CSOs and the media.
   (c) Dissemination of the dashboard by the "Family Guides" and information sessions for the
       beneficiaries on the improvement they should expect.
   (d) Implementation of community base monitoring activities of dashboard
   (e) Carry out periodical progress-report meetings among the public institutions that signed
       the agreements to tackle obstacles and mitigate risks related to goals reflected on the
       dashboard.
   (f) Carry out information and dissemination sessions of progress made in improving
       Tekoporii.

Component 3: Knowledge management for program transparency, improvement and
sustainability and project management.

   (b) Develop a data base containing multi-media contents generated by the different

II. Key Safeguard Policy Issues and Their Management
A. Summary ofKey Safeguard Issues
 I. Describe any safeguard issues and impacts associated with the proposed project. Identify and
describe any potential large scale, significant and/or irreversible impacts:
The project will not finance any physical activities. No risks related to environmental or social
safeguards policies are anticipated during implementation of project activities. Targeted areas
have some presence of Indigenous Peoples; however, these families are not targeted directly by
the CCT program (Tekopora). The Government has another social protection program that
includes conditional cash transfers for indigenous population ("Plan Integral para los Pueblos
Indigenas"). If households with presence of indigenous population are included in the sample,
CIRD has agreed to ensure that all relevant documentation and information will be translated to
Guarani to facilitate the participation of these households, including the use of Guarani speaking
surveyors.

5. Identify the key stakeholders and describe the mechanisms for consultation and disclosure on
safeguard policies, with an emphasis on potentially affected people:

The Project's direct beneficiaries include: 88,000 extreme poor households, in 15 of the 17
provinces, in 90 out of the 240 existing municipalities in the country. These households are
already part ofTekopora CCT Program and will benefit from the immediate improvements in the
program and from better understanding their responsibilities regarding the compliance of
conditions.

The Project's indirect beneficiaries include: (I) Paraguayan citizens, who will benefit from
success of the program as it will increase the socio-economic opportunities of 200,000 extreme
poor families, enabling them to better contribute to the overall development of Paraguay; (2)
SAS, who will benefit from the information as it will help to strengthen the management of the
program; deliver better results; increase support for the expansion of the program; and provide
sound information for budget allocation and negotiation; (3) MEC and the MSPBS, who will
benefit from better understanding the gaps in the supply side of the program, to better allocate
budget and improve policy decisions; (4) Ministry of Finance and government agencies, who
will use the information to improve budget allocation and to better monitor the use of funds
linked to Tekopora.
